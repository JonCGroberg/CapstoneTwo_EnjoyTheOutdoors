Frequently Asked Questions

How much do national parks cost to visit?

Most national parks have an entry fee per vehicle or person that provides access for up to a week. Fees are typically $30 or less. Some parks offer annual passes for unlimited entry.

When is the busiest time to visit?

The peak summer season from June-August tends to be most crowded. Some parks like Glacier and Rocky Mountain see heavy traffic on summer weekends too. Consider visiting spring and fall for smaller crowds.

Do I need reservations for everything?

Campground reservations may be needed at popular parks but not always for day use. Check individual park pages for details on bookings for campgrounds, backcountry permits or special tours/programs.

Can you spot wildlife easily?

It depends on the park and location, but wildlife sightings usually require some patience. Early mornings and evenings tend to be best. Come prepared with binoculars. Rangers can advise on hot spots too.
